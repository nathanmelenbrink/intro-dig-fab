Electronics Basics and Microcontroller Intro.

Agenda:

Weekly Overview
3:15-4:00: Assignment/Website Review and continued introductions. Science Center Lab Website
4:00-4:30: Introduction to microcontrollers and Introduction to Electronics and test equipment.
Circuit Simulation with Tinkercad and the Falstad applet.
Gathering tools and components. Safety discussion.

  • Metro M0 Microcontroller
  • Breadboard
  • Wires and stripper
  • Multimeter
  • Resistors and potentiometer (bag 1)
  • One or more LEDs (bag 3)
4:30-5:00: Breakout rooms for basic circuits.
  • Use a breadboard.
  • Build an LED circuit.
  • Use a multimeter to measure voltage.
  • Find the current through the LED.
  • Calculate Power and discuss energy.
5:00-5:15: Break. Install Arduino IDE.

5:15-5:30: Demonstration of Metro M0 board and Blink.

5:30 - 6:00: Breakout rooms: get Metro M0 installed, run the Blink program.
  • Blink with built-in LED
  • Blink with an LED on the breadboard.
  • Microcontroller as a voltmeter: ADC (Analog to Digital Converter, or "Analog in") and Serial.

Lab Sections

Use the Electronics basics. page and its links. Use information posted in Slack #electronics channel.
Use the resistor color code.
Use the multimeter in Voltage, resistance, and continuity modes.
Build a voltage divider circuit. Build a potentiometer circuit.
Build and measure circuits with resistors in parallel and series.
Make sure you can load programs into your Metro M0 board. Use example programs "blink" and "AnalogReadSerial"
Continue to work with the Microcontroller ADC and explore sensors like the thermistor and magnetic field sensor. See Sensor webpage.
Explore other components: Capacitors, diodes, sensors.

Readings:

In preparation for the next topic, read chapter 8 of Making Things Move. Make a note of some of the more interesting points you learn.

Assignment:

  • Make a circuit that does something (motion or light). Use a multimeter to measure the behavior of your circuit.
  • Simulate your circuit.
  • Use the Microcontroller Analog in (ADC) to measure the voltage of a potentiometer, or other voltage-divider-based sensor.
  • Document your work and learning.
